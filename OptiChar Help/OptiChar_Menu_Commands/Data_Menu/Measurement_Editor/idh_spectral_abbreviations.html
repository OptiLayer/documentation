Spectral Characteristics Abbreviations

In OptiChar, you can specify a spectral characteristic using its character abbreviations. They are as follows:

  • R - Reflectance

  • BR - Back Reflectance

  • T - Transmittance

  • A - Absorptance

  • PSI - Ellipsometric angle Psi

  • DEL - Ellipsometric angle Delta

  • DELF - Ellipsometric angle Delta folded

  • TANP - Tan(Psi)

  • COSD - Cos(Delta)

  • DPOL - Depolarization

  • GDR - Group delay on reflection

  • GDDR - Group delay dispersion on reflection

  • GDT - Group delay on transmission

  • GDDT - Group delay dispersion on transmission

All of these abbreviations (with the exception of PSI, DEL, DELF, TANP, COSD, DPOL) should be accompanied by the polarization state abbreviations:

  • S, P - For completely polarized light;

  • A - For unpolarized light.

Note: The angle of incidence is always specified in the Incident Medium. For back reflectance and similar characteristics, this means that the angle of the Back Reflectance should be computed using Snell’s Law if the Exit Medium is different from the Incident one.
