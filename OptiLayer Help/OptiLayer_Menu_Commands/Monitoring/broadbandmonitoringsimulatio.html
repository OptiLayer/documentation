Broadband Monitoring Simulation

` <pre_production_monochromatic.html>`__ ` <idh_menu_analysis.html>`__ ` <broadmonsim_deprates.html>`__

Broadband Monitoring Simulation option is one of the special OptiLayer options aimed at performing experiments on computational manufacturing of optical coatings. Experiments on computational manufacturing are much cheaper and faster than real deposition experiments. They can be used for comparing the feasibility properties of different theoretical designs, studying how parameters of deposition processes and monitoring devices influence the quality of production, revealing critical layers whose thicknesses should be monitored with special care, and much more.

You can find more information on this subject in the paper:

A. V. Tikhonravov and M. K. Trubetskov, “Computational manufacturing as a bridge between design and production,” Appl. Opt. 44, 6877-6884 (2005)
http://www.opticsinfobase.org/abstract.cfm?URI=ao-44-32-6877

The Broadband Monitoring Simulation option allows the user to perform computational experiments simulating deposition processes in vacuum chambers equipped with broadband monitoring devices. A six-step dialog is used to specify parameters of the deposition process and monitoring device, run computational experiments, and analyze their results.
