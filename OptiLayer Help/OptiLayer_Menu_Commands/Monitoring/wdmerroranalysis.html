WDM Monitoring Simulation

` <single_wavelength_result.html>`__ ` <idh_menu_analysis.html>`__ ` <wdm_error_analysis_st1.html>`__

The WDM Error Analysis is based on the simulation of the WDM filter deposition with turning point optical monitoring at the filter central wavelength. Due to this type of monitoring, the effect of errors self-compensation is employed in the WDM filter production. During simulated deposition runs, errors in layer thicknesses are not intentionally introduced but originate from simulated actual error sources, including the instability of deposition rates, random noise, and drifts in measured transmittance data.

We recommend reading the papers:

  • A. Tikhonravov, M. K. Trubetskov, and A. V. Voronov, “Automated monitoring of WDM filters,” in Optical Interference Coatings, OSA Technical Digest Series (Optical Society of America, 2001), paper WA3. ` <http://www.opticsinfobase.org/abstract.cfm?URI=OIC-2001-WA3>`__/ http://www.opticsinfobase.org/abstract.cfm?URI=OIC-2001-WA3

  • A. V. Tikhonravov and M. K. Trubetskov, “Automated Design and Sensitivity Analysis of Wavelengh-Division Multiplexing Filters,” Appl. Opt. 41, 3176-3182 (2002) http://www.opticsinfobase.org/abstract.cfm?URI=ao-41-16-3176

for a detailed discussion of error self-compensation effect in connection with the automated monitoring of WDM filters.
