Layer Material Status

In order to control the manner in which a material behaves during the Needle Optimization process, it is assigned a material status state. There are four possible states that may be assigned to a material: Active (A), Inactive (I), Passive (P), and Exclusionary (E). The default state assigned to each material when it is loaded is Active. During the Needle Optimization process:

  • An Active material may be inserted into any other layer in the design, and any other material designated as Active or Exclusionary may be inserted into it.

  • An Inactive material may not be inserted into any other layer in the design, nor may any other material be inserted into it.

  • A Passive material may not be inserted into any other layer in the design, but any other material designated as Active or Exclusionary may be inserted into it.

  • An Exclusionary material may be inserted into any other layer in the design, but no other material may be inserted into it.

It is also possible to use the Arrange Materials menu item from the Data menu. Note that the material state assignments are global; thus, changes made in the Synthesis Options menu also affect the Analysis Options, and vice versa. A quick way to open the same dialog is to use the Arrange Materials command from the Data menu.
