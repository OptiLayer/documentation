Design Database

OptiLayer Design essentially comprises a collection of data that details the sequence and physical characteristics of a set of layers. Each layer is defined by its thickness, the material from which it is made, and its status. The material is denoted by an abbreviation for ease of reference. Using abbreviations to represent layer materials enables the design structure to be independent of the specific materials of the layers. In the system, each material is assigned an abbreviation, which can be modified via the Materials tab in the Options dialog.

Layer thicknesses can be designated in several units: metric, optical, Quarter-Wave (QWOT), or Full-Wave (FWOT). Once a design and the corresponding materials for the abbreviations used in the design are loaded into the system, an automatic association is maintained among these four types of thickness measurements. It’s important to note that for QWOT and FWOT units, the control wavelength must be defined in the design document. The control wavelength serves as a crucial parameter for establishing a relationship between the physical and the optical thickness of a layer. This parameter should not be confused with the Reference Wavelength found in the Configuration dialog (under the Thickness Units tab), which is solely used for displaying the thicknesses of layers.

Layer status enables users to dictate whether a layer’s thickness is variable or fixed during the optimization process. Thicknesses with an active status (indicated as “A”) can be adjusted throughout the optimization process, whereas those with a fixed status (“F”) remain constant. The default status is “active”. Users can modify this parameter by editing the respective entry in the design document’s right-hand column.

Important Notes:

If the specific materials corresponding to the character abbreviations in a design file are changed, the optical thicknesses of the layers in a design will remain constant, while only the physical (metric) thicknesses of the layers will adjust.

To maintain the physical thicknesses unchanged, it is essential to activate the “Keep physical thicknesses” option located at the bottom of the Design Editor dialog.

For more details on how to alter design parameters, refer to the Modifying Design section.

At the bottom of the Design Editor, summary information is displayed. This summary includes the total physical thickness, total optical thickness, total Full-Wave Optical Thickness (FWOT), total Quarter-Wave Optical Thickness (QWOT), and the total number of layers. A more detailed report on the design can be obtained by using the “Σ” button.
