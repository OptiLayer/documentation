Needle Optimization

` <sensitivity_directed_refinemen.html>`__ ` <idh_menu_synthesis.html>`__ ` <idh_needle_optimization_dialog.html>`__

 Needle optimization is a unique feature of OptiLayer and is especially useful for solving complicated design problems. Needle optimization involves inserting new layers into the optimized design, typically thin needle-like variations of the refractive index profile. During the needle optimization process, a plot of a special function (the P-function) is displayed on the screen, with the best insertion point being where the P-function has its largest negative value. In automatic mode, the program automatically identifies this point and computes an optimal thickness for the new layer. The material for a new layer inserted during the needle optimization process is selected based on certain rules. In a two-component coating, the material for the new layer differs from the material of the existing layer where the insertion occurs. For coatings with more than two materials, the optimal material is chosen from those designated as Active (A) or Exclusionary (E) in the Materials window.

Needle Optimization AUTO

If you start the needle optimization process by choosing the Needle optimization AUTO option, an automatic procedure is invoked. This involves a series of alternating needle variation and refinement steps. The process continues until it is no longer possible to insert a new layer that would enhance the current design. This stopping criterion is based on either a negligible improvement in the merit function or obtaining a positive P-function value within all layers of the current design. This indicates that an optimal solution for a coating with the current total optical thickness has been achieved. Further enhancement of the merit function would require increasing the total optical thickness of the design (refer to Gradual Evolution).

You can track the progress of the optimization procedure in the Needle Variation window and in the Refinement window (when Silent mode is disabled).

Needle Optimization Deep Search

The Deep Search variant of Needle Optimization should be used in situations where you are interested in a design with good performance but have a strict limitation on the number of layers. During each major iteration, the Deep Search version attempts all possible needle insertions and subsequent refinements one by one. The achieved reductions in the merit function are analyzed, and the insertion variant that provides the largest decrease in the merit function is selected. The Deep Search variant is time-consuming and is recommended only for specific problems. Its advantages become evident in rather complex problems (involving more than 50 layers, intricate targets with phase, GD, GDD, etc., requirements).
