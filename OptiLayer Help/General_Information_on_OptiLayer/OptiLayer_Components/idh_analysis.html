Analysis Tools

OptiLayer’s Analysis Tools

In OptiLayer, the Analysis menu offers a range of commands that allow users to calculate various parameters related to the optical performance of the design currently loaded in memory.

The Evaluation  commands enable users to compute and plot a diverse set of characteristics, including:

  • Reflectance, transmittance, and absorptance for s- and p-polarized light as well as non-polarized light.

  • Phase shifts on reflection and transmission for s- and p-polarizations.

  • Differential phase shifts on reflection and transmission.

  • Group delay (GD) and group delay dispersion (GDD) for the transmitted and reflected light.

  • Ellipsometric angles psi and delta.

These characteristics can be evaluated over user-defined spectral and angle of incidence ranges, allowing for detailed analysis of the optical performance of the designed coatings. By leveraging the Analysis menu and its Evaluation commands in OptiLayer, users can gain insights into critical optical parameters and assess the performance of their coatings under different conditions.

In OptiLayer, the Evaluation modes of the program offer additional convenient options to enhance the user experience and facilitate analysis. These options include:

  • Averaging coating characteristics over arbitrary spectral or angle of incidence ranges: This feature allows users to obtain average values of the evaluated parameters over specified spectral or angle ranges, providing a consolidated view of the coating performance within those ranges.

  • Selecting exact numerical values from evaluation plots at arbitrary points: Users can interact with the evaluation plots to extract precise numerical data from specific points of interest, enabling detailed analysis and validation of the results.

  • Configuring the number of evaluation plots and their chart appearance: OptiLayer allows users to customize the number of evaluation plots displayed and adjust their visual appearance for an optimized viewing experience. This flexibility ensures that users can tailor the presentation of data to their preferences and analysis requirements.

These convenient options within the Evaluation modes empower users to efficiently analyze coating characteristics, extract specific data points for further examination, and customize the visual representation of evaluation results to suit their needs.

The Color analysis command in OptiLayer facilitates the examination of color coordinates and other crucial color parameters using color diagrams or convenient spreadsheet presentations.

Users can calculate color coordinates of a coating with an arbitrary light source in the reflected or transmitted light of any polarization. Luminous reflectance and transmittance can also be calculated with arbitrary light sources and states of polarization.

The Field command computes and displays the electric field within the coating layers. It offers various options for users to analyze the electric field distribution based on factors such as the wavelength of incident light and angle of incidence.

The Admittance command calculates and displays admittance diagrams, allowing users to study the admittance diagram based on factors like the wavelength of incident light and angle of incidence.

The Refractive Index Profile command provides a visual representation of the refractive index profile across the design layers.

Zeros and Poles is an advanced feature in OptiLayer that allows users to evaluate parameters relevant to modern technology applications that require group delay and group delay dispersion specifications.

The WDM Filter GD Performance option enables users to explore the relationship between the transmission band shape and group delay properties, which is significant for developments related to 40-Gb/s applications.

The Rugates feature allows users to create rugate-type multilayer coatings described by formula expressions for further analysis. This feature enhances the versatility and analytical capabilities of OptiLayer in designing and evaluating complex multilayer coatings.

OptiLayer offers a range of options tailored for detailed pre-production analysis of designed optical coatings.

The Error Analysis The Error Analysis command in OptiLayer enables users to investigate the impact of errors on the spectral characteristics of optical coatings. This analysis can be conducted specifically for errors in layer thickness and errors in refractive indices.

  • Errors in Layer Thickness:

    • By introducing variations in the thickness of individual layers within the coating, users can assess how these deviations affect the spectral properties of the coating.

    • Analyzing errors in layer thickness helps users understand the sensitivity of the coating to variations in layer thickness and can guide adjustments to minimize the impact of manufacturing tolerances.

  • Errors in Refractive Indices:

    • Evaluating errors in the refractive indices of the materials used in the coating allows users to explore the influence of refractive index variations on the optical performance of the coating.

    • Understanding the impact of errors in refractive indices is essential for optimizing the design and manufacturing processes to ensure that the coating meets the desired specifications.

By performing error analysis for layer thickness and refractive indices, users can effectively assess the robustness and reliability of their optical coating designs, enabling them to make informed decisions to enhance the performance and manufacturability of the coatings.

The Worst Case Analysis command in OptiLayer generates plots showing corridors of maximum deviations of spectral characteristics based on specified levels of errors in coating parameters. By visualizing these worst-case scenarios, users can understand the potential variability in spectral performance and optimize the design and manufacturing processes accordingly.

The Layer Sensitivity command ranks the sensitivity of design layers to errors in their thickness, providing relative sensitivity numbers for all design layers. This analysis helps users identify which layers have a significant impact on the overall coating performance and prioritize adjustments to minimize sensitivity to thickness variations.

The Systematic Deviation command allows users to analyze the effects of systematic deviations of coating parameters that may result from inaccuracies in monitoring calibration or other sources. By examining these systematic deviations, users can identify potential sources of error and optimize the coating design and manufacturing processes to improve overall accuracy and consistency.

The Interface Roughness command enables users to estimate the impact of interface roughness on the spectral characteristics of multilayer coatings. Understanding the effects of interface roughness can help users optimize coating designs to minimize the impact of roughness on optical performance.

The Inhomogeneities/Interlayers command allows users to estimate the combined effects of inhomogeneities and interface layers on the spectral characteristics of multilayer coatings. By evaluating these combined effects, users can enhance the accuracy and reliability of their coating designs to meet desired performance specifications. These advanced analysis tools in OptiLayer provide users with comprehensive insights to optimize the design and manufacturing processes of optical coatings.

In OptiLayer, the Pre-Production Estimation of Errors commands for Broadband Monitoring and Monochromatic Monitoring allow users to assess the impact of errors on the optical monitoring procedure in different monitoring scenarios. These commands help users analyze potential errors and optimize monitoring strategies for both broadband and monochromatic monitoring applications. Below are the key features of these commands:

  • Pre-Production Estimation of Errors - Broadband Monitoring

  • Pre-Production Estimation of Errors - Monochromatic Monitoring

By utilizing these Pre-Production Estimation of Errors commands for Broadband Monitoring and Monochromatic Monitoring in OptiLayer, users can gain a comprehensive understanding of the potential errors that may arise during the monitoring process and develop effective strategies to minimize errors and improve coating quality. These commands empower users to proactively address monitoring challenges and enhance the accuracy and reliability of the optical monitoring procedure for optimal coating performance.

The WDM Error Analysis command in OptiLayer is designed for conducting a detailed pre-production analysis of Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM) filters by simulating the deposition process with thickness control based on turning point optical monitoring. In real-world deposition processes, errors in layer thickness can occur due to inaccuracies in determining turning points, which may be influenced by factors such as instability of deposition rates, random noise in monitoring data, drifts in monitoring data over time, etc. The WDM Error Analysis option allows users to investigate the impact of these factors on the quality and performance of WDM filters.
