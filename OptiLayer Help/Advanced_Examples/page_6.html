The main purpose of this example is to demonstrate the evaluation of the intensity and phase spectral properties of QWM. In parallel with this demonstration, it is shown how the designs in the RAM can be modified and stored to the design database. The introduction of the phase of the amplitude reflection coefficient in the p-case is also discussed. It is shown that the p-phase in OptiLayer is introduced in the most logical way.

In the File -> Project menu of OptiLayer, open the directory LEC24D2. The datafiles needed to run this exercise are already loaded into RAM. These are the substrate Glass n=1.52, the layer materials n=2.35 and n=1.46, and the design QWM-19, c.w. 600 nm.

Open the T&R&Phase option in the Analysis menu. The transmittance of the 19-layer QWM is displayed in the spectral range of 400 to 800 nm.

To check the change in mirror transmittance when changing the number of mirror layer, go to the Synthesis -> Modify Design option and increase the number of design layers from 19 to 25.

Check that the design spreadsheet now has 25 rows. After clicking OK, the changes are applied to the design in RAM.

 Note that the steepness of the transmittance slopes at the boundaries of the high reflection region has increased considerably.

To save the modified mirror design to the design database stored on the hard disk, select Data -> Save Data -> Save Design option. Give the saved design a name and click OK. You can now see the new design datafile in the Design database window.

Load the original 19-layer QWM into RAM. Let us study the dependence of the mirror spectral characteristics on the angle of incidence. For oblique light incidence, we should consider characteristics for two polarization states, s- and p-states. Right-click in the evaluation window and select the Plot -> Plots options.

Click the Add button to add new characteristics to display. Let us choose reflectances and phase shifts on reflection for the s- and p- polarized light at 45-degree light incidence (four plots). Use different colors for s- and p- characteristics, solid and dashed curves for intensity and phase characteristics.

We see that the phase shifts on reflection for the s- and p-polarized light are the same at the wavelength in the center of the high reflection zone. As the incidence tends toward normal incidence, the s- and p-phases approach each other in a wide wavelength range and then coincide at normal incidence. This can be checked by changing the incidence angles to smaller values (for example, 5-10 degrees) in the Plot options. Of course, this is to be expected. But in some texts, the phase shifts on reflection for the s- and p-polarized light differ by 180 degrees at normal incidence.

This difference is caused by the different introduction of the reflection phase in the p-case compared to OptiLayer. The example discussed shows that the p-phase definition used in OptiLayer is the most natural.
