Example 11. Optimization with random starting designs: hot mirrors are used as example.

` <ex ercise15_metal_dielectric.htm>`__ ` <idh_optilayer.htm>`__ ` <example1.htm>`__

 Note: Due to the continuous improvement of OptiLayer design options and random nature of some of their elements, the results obtained may differ from those previously saved in the design database.

The main purpose of this example is to demonstrate the Random optimization … mode, which finds designs through multiple optimizations with random starting designs.

In the File -> Project menu of OptiLayer, open the directory LEC24D11. Datafiles needed to run this exercise are already loaded into RAM. These are the substrate Glass n=1.52, the layer materials n=2.35 and n=1.46, and the Hot mirror target. This target specifies requirements for 100% transmittance in the 400-690 nm spectral range and 0% transmittance in the 710-1200 nm spectral range. The target transmittance can be viewed in the Preview tab of the Target editor.

In the previous LEC24D10 example, a gradual evolution design procedure was used to design the required hot mirrors. Elimination of thin layers in designs has been shown to be challenging. The design option considered in this example allows the application of lower and upper constraints on the thicknesses of all coating layers. It will be shown that constrained optimization with random starting designs will allow us to obtain designs without thin layers and better merit function values than that of the design obtained in the previous example after removing thin layers.

In the Synthesis menu, select the Randon Optimization… option. The window for setting random optimization parameters will open. This window has two tabs: Global and Local. The Global tab allows you to run the option with starting designs generated in entire area specified by the Min and Max parameters in the window, and the Local tab sets parameters for selecting starting designs in some vicinity to a given design.

** **

It is generally recommended to start the design procedure from the Global tab options. The results of the gradual evolution procedure in LEC24D10 give us a general idea of the number of layers and total optical thickness of the design required to achieve the desired level of approximation of the hot mirror target transmittance. We will set 40 in The number of layers field and set the average optical thicknesses of H and L materials to 200 nm. This will ensure that the total optical thicknesses of starting designs are close to the value of 8000 nm set for calculations in LEC24D10. The lower and upper limits (Min and Max fields) on the optical thicknesses of all layers will be 100 nm and 350 nm, respectively.

Make sure that in the Materials nearest to the substrate field the abbreviation H for the high index layer is set.

Click the OK button and go to the main window of the Random Optimization… option. Clicking the Run button starts the design procedure. The progress of the design procedure is reflected on the left side of the window, and the MF, N, and TOT parameters of the final designs obtained using randomly generated starting designs are shown on the right side of the window. The results obtained are sorted in accordance with the merit functions values achieved at each optimization procedure. You can terminate the design procedure at any time by clicking the Stop button.

You can select any design with the parameters listed on the right side of the window and use the Load button to move it to the General Information window and T&R&Phase window. The design can then be saved in the design database.

Alternatively, it may be worth searching for a better design in the vicinity of the selected design. To do this, click the Parameters button and switch to the Local tab at the top of the parameters window. If this tab is active, further optimization procedures will be performed using the starting designs from the vicinity of the selected design. This vicinity is determined by the related parameters set in the RMS or Relative RMS columns. Click the OK button and then the Run button in the main window to continue optimization. Often the best results are achieved this way.

It may be worth running random optimization using different local optimization methods, since it is impossible to predict which one will work best for your specific project.

Sample hot mirror designs HM1 and HM2 are saved in the design database.
