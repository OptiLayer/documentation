Example 22. Low-E (low emissivity) coatings for glazing applications.

 Note: Due to the continuous improvement of OptiLayer options and random nature of some of their elements, the results obtained may differ from those described below.

This example presents an analysis of the spectral and color properties of optical coatings used to reduce heat transfer in double and triple glazing. We use materials typical for such coatings, with refractive index wavelength dependencies taken from the open access database Refractive Index Info. In particular, the refractive index NiCr is modeled based on the Ni and Cr data, assuming 80% - 20% nickel and chromium content.

In the File -> Project menu of OptiLayer, open the directory LEC24D22. Datafiles needed to run this exercise are already loaded into RAM. These are the substrate Soda lime glass, the layer materials Si3N4, Ag, and NiCr, the design Double silver low-E, and the target AR 420-680 nm + low-E 900-1800 nm. Open the T&R&Phase window to see the spectral target and normal incidence transmittance of the loaded design. The loaded design is a 9-layer coating with layer thicknesses set in the ranges specified in the above-mentioned patent.

The loaded materials have abbreviations H, A, and N for the Si3N4, Ag, and NiCr, respectively.

 Note: that the design has two Ag layers of 11 and 15 nm thickness and four very thin NiCr layers with the thicknesses of 0.7 nm each. These NiCr layers are used to improve adhesion at the boundaries of the Ag and nearby Si3N4 layers.

 Note that due to the presence of two silver layers, the 9-layer design under consideration has a low transmittance in the IR region, and, accordingly, a low thermal emissivity. In the most part of the specified AR region, it has a transmittance of over 70%.

To analyze the color properties of this design, go to the Analysis -> Color… option and in the Color Evaluation Parameters window, check Transmittance and Reflectance in the Characteristic field.

Click the Calculate button at the bottom of this window. The Color Evaluation window will open with the CIE xyz (1931) chromaticity diagram. It shows that the color of the transmitted light is close to white, and the color of the reflected light is in the blue color range.

It is worth doing a couple of experiments with the 9-layer design. First, we will try to improve the transmittance in the AR region while keeping the NiCr layer thicknesses at 0.7 nm. Use the Edit command in the design database to see the design spreadsheet of the Double silver low-E design.

Note that in the Status column of the spreadsheet, all NiCr layers have the status F, which means that their thicknesses will be kept unchanged during the refinement procedure.

Go to the Synthesis -> Refinement option and click the Run button to start the refinement procedure. The transmittance in the AR region has increased slightly, but this increase is hardly significant.

Now let us load the Double silver low-E design again. Go to the Modify Design option and change the status of all NiCr layer to A (active).

Open the Refinement option again and run the refinement procedure. A significant improvement in transmittance in the AR region is observed. Note that this improvement was achieved by removing all NiCr layers from the design structure. The resulting design has only 5 layers. It is saved in the design database under the name Double silver low-E refined NiCr not fixed.

It is worth loading this design and checking its color properties. They are close to the color properties of the original 9-layer design.
